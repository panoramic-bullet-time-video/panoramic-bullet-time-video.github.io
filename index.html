(a) The state-of-the-art perspective camera array developed by our joint group with Huawei, which observes the target from an outside-in view, can only focus on a limited number of targets (green) within a narrow public FoV. Abstract

Bullet-time video, a significant application of free-viewpoint video technology, offers an immersive viewing experience by allowing the camera viewpoint to switch along the camera array orbit. This technology is widely used in film, television, and broadcasting. However, current bullet-time videos are generated by perspective cameras with a limited field of view (FoV), which narrows the common FoV of the camera array. This limitation can reduce target selection interactivity and result in low immersion for the produced bullet-time video. To address these limitations, we propose using multiple 360° panoramic cameras to construct a panoramic camera array for bullet-time video synthesis. The panoramic camera array enables complete coverage of the scene and introduces additional rotational degrees of freedom (DoF). Using the panoramic camera array, we propose a bullet-time video synthesis method based on the target axis to enhance the smoothness of the bullet-time video. This is achieved by maintaining the position, direction, and size of the target axis stable in the output bullet-time video frame. In addition, we propose a novel interactive high-dimensional bullet-time video synthesis system. The system incorporates a comprehensive pipeline that includes modules for panoramic video capture, panoramic camera array calibration, panoramic video codec, and bullet-time video synthesis. In our system, users can interactively select any target in the full scene and enjoy higher-DoF immersive bullet-time videos in real-time on lightweight devices like laptops. Qualitative and quantitative evaluations on simulated and real datasets demonstrate that our method outperforms state-of-the-art methods. In addition, our system was demonstrated at the 2023 Hangzhou Asian Games.

System overview

Figure 2. Our interactive high-dimensional bullet-time video synthesis system pipeline. The system primarily comprises four modules. (a) First, we construct a camera array using multiple panoramic cameras and capture the panoramic videos. (b) Then, we conduct multi-camera calibration to estimate the poses of the panoramic cameras. (c) Next, we decode the multiple panoramic videos into panoramas. (d) Finally, the target AB is stabilized in the views through our bullet-time video synthesis method. (e) Users can interactively select any targets within the entire scene to enjoy smooth and stable bullet-time videos.

Bullet-time video synthesis method

Figure 3. Our bullet-time video synthesis method. For a panoramic camera array, users can interactively select the target axis. By setting the extrinsic parameters and intrinsic parameters of the virtual camera, the position, direction, and size of the target axis in the output virtual frames can be stable. Users can enjoy smooth bullet-time video by switching the virtual views.

Dataset

Each dataset contains multi-viewpoint panoramas and corresponding calibration parameters files, where the image resolution is 8192×4096, and the camera pose is defined from the world coordinate system to the camera coordinate system. You can download our data through Google Drive or Baidu Netdisk.
